Essay on Pollution

Pollution refers to the introduction of harmful substances into the environment, resulting in adverse effects on living beings and the planet. It can take many forms, including air, water, soil, and noise pollution.

Air pollution is caused by emissions from vehicles, industries, and burning of fossil fuels. Water pollution results from the dumping of industrial waste, sewage, and chemicals into water bodies. Soil pollution is caused by excessive use of pesticides, deforestation, and improper waste disposal. Noise pollution stems from loud sounds produced by machinery, transportation, and urban activities.

To combat pollution, we must adopt eco-friendly practices, reduce waste, use renewable energy sources, and enforce strict environmental regulations. Public awareness and active participation are crucial in preserving our environment for future generations.
